# Men should read women writers

There are many reasons why men should read more of women writers. I'll write about one:

We were told what behaviours/traits women would like in men, mostly by male writers.  The ‘Alpha’ ones off late. And boys grow up mimicing a lot of these behaviours. I’m lucky to have grown-up between a bunch of women who negated such influence on me.

I read a short story ‘Zikora’ by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie and there’s a passage in which she describes what she liked in her boyfriend. I felt more boys should read such writings. 


> He had a boyish quality, which was not, as in some men, mere cover for immaturity;  he  was  a  grown-up  who could  still  touch  in  himself the wonder and innocence of childhood.


The story touches upon many other themes like pregnancy, inheritance, sex-education, etc. It is better to know about them from women writers.

> Nature must not want humans to reproduce, otherwise birthing would be easy, even enjoyable: babies would easily slip out, and mothers would remain unmarked and whole, merely blessed by having bestowed life.


It makes for a good woman’s day read, especially for men!
