# Uncertainty is God

My mom asked me to take a copy of her Aadhar card using the new HP Printer-Copy-Scan we got at home. My aunt, generally curious about new machines, joined me. 

The first time I tried, I printed Photo-ID and Address on either side of paper. 

One paper got wasted.

I tried again by switching the paper in tray. I printed it reverse.

I got irritated. I placed the card in opposite direction and then tried for the third time. As the machine drew the paper in and started printing, my aunt mumbled, “దేవుడా ! దేవుడా! ఈసరి పని చెయ్యాలి!” (God, God, It has to work this time!)

Two failed attempts with a photo-copier made my aunt pray.

I printed it right this time.

Similarly, my mom prays whenever I travel to Bengaluru overnight by bus. 

Obviously, God has no role play in getting a photo-copy right or in helping me reach Bengaluru safe.

But given the uncertainty of outcomes, and silence of those responsible for outcomes, we are left with the God.

*And taming uncertainty is thus the most spiritual thing one could do.*
