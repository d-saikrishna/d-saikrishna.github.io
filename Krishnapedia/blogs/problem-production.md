# The problem of production (& consumption)

I’m re-reading _Small is Beautiful_ and I feel it has some useful models to think about economy and life in general.

<div style="text-align: center"><img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/S/compressed.photo.goodreads.com/books/1770070208i/1117634.jpg" style="width: 100%; max-width: 300px;"/></div>


In the first chapter, Schumacher diagnoses that the _problem of production (and our consumption)_ is not yet solved as we want to delude ourselves. 

He takes a simple logic to explain this and it is relatable to any middle class mind. What do you call someone in your homes who lives off family assets? Unless he/she is on retirement, we would call them **_irresponsible._** Even a retiree attempts to live-off the rental and interest income from their capital assets so that assets remain for the next generation.

We are failing to apply this intuitive logic in the economy. We are living-off by eroding the capital assets: natural resources. We all are **_irresponsible._**

The solution is to produce (and thus consume) in such a way that the act of production preserves and promotes the capital. 

We should do agriculture in such a way that soil quality is preserved, along with producing food.

We should do industry in such a way that worker quality should be improved, along with producing goods.

We all re-invest the interest from our FDs to increase the size of the FD. Compounding! We know this logic. We should just apply it on a wider scale.
