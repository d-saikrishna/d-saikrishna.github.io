# A critique of elite environmentalism
<div style="text-align: center"><img src="https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/news/1024/cpsprodpb/A5B8/production/_104142424_gettyimages-459129782.jpg.webp" style="width: 100%; max-width: 500px;"/></div>

This is a critique of American environmentalism by Ramachandra Guha. We can generalise it as elite environmentalism as many in India also subscribe to this form of environmentalism.

The issue is that the elite environmentalists don't consider the life-style changes required to protect the environment. 

> For most Americans it is perfectly consistent to drive a thousand miles to spend a holiday in a national park

The consumerism that has led us here is not questioned. Nature just becomes a matter of 'quality of life', a good-to-have.

> "If we are concerned about our great appetite for materials, it is plausible to seek to increase the supply, to decrease waste, to make better use of the stocks available, and to develop substitutes. But what of the appetite itself?" -- JK Galbraith
