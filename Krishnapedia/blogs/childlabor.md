In this scene from the movie “The Young Karl Marx”, Karl Marx discusses child labor with an English industrialist. 

The industrialist argues that child labour is necessary as it keeps the labour costs low — as there’d be more supply in the labor market (supply-demand dynamics). He further says that without child labour, he’d go bankrupt. 
> there’d be no more profits, no more economy and thus no more society.

Now, fortunately, we are not living in an economy or society whose survival is dependent on children working day and night in factories. We want them in schools and parks instead. Don’t we?

How did we build this society?

The answer is in values. We learnt to believe that children should not be labour — even if it reduces the efficiency of the labor market. Yes, labor costs increase when children are not in labour supply. But, we realised that there are other things more beautiful in life, beyond markets. And this realisation need not stop at child labour. 🌳🚰🦚🏔️🏝️

> What you call profits is what I call exploitation
