# Honesty vs Integrity
<div style="text-align: center"><img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/51n3NwPPw7L._SY445_SX342_ML2_.jpg" style="width: 100%; max-width: 500px;"/></div>


In his memoir, "Surely, You're joking, Mr.Feynman!", Richard Feynman distinguished between honesty and integrity when it comes to making scientific claims. He spoke of an advertisement which claimed that a certain branded `Wesson Oil` doesn’t soak through food. That was an honest claim, but it lacked scientific integrity. 

> The fact that should be added to that advertising statement is that no oils soak through food, if operated at a certain temperature. If operated at another temperature, they all will— including Wesson oil.


> The first principle is that you must not fool yourself— and you are the easiest person to fool.
